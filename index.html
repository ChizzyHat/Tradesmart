How Markets Actually Work
Weeks 1 & 2  ·  Foundation
Foundation ▼

Before touching any chart, the goal is to understand what the market actually is -- so it never feels like a mystery machine that spits out money or takes it away randomly.

Week 1
What Are Stocks, Crypto, and Markets?
  • Learn what a stock is -- you are buying a tiny piece of a company
  • Learn what crypto is -- digital currency with no central bank backing it
  • Understand what "the market" means -- buyers and sellers agreeing on price
  • Set up a free TradingView account and explore the interface for 15 minutes
  • Pick 3 companies you actually know (Apple, Nike, Tesla) and find them on TradingView
  • Watch their price for the week -- just observe, no analysis yet
⚡Short bursts work best. 15 minutes of focused reading beats an hour of zoning out. Set a timer, take a break, come back.
Week 2
Supply, Demand, and Why Prices Move
  • Learn the basic rule: more buyers than sellers = price goes up. More sellers than buyers = price goes down
  • Study one real news event and find it on a chart -- did the price move when the news hit?
  • Learn what a bull market and bear market mean
  • Set up TradingView paper trading account -- start with $20,000 in virtual money
  • Make your first 2 paper trades just to learn the interface. This is practice, not strategy yet.
Checkpoint 01  ·  End of Week 2
Before moving to Phase 2, you must be able to:
  • Explain in your own words what a stock is and what makes its price move
  • Find any company on TradingView and pull up its chart
  • Have made at least 2 paper trades (no strategy required -- just know how the buttons work)
  • Have a trading journal started with at least 5 entries

Reading Charts Like a Pro
Weeks 3 & 4  ·  Technical Basics
Foundation 🔒 ▼
This phase is locked. Complete Phase 1 and have a parent enter the password to continue.

Charts are the language of trading. You don't need to know all of them -- just a handful, done well.

Week 3
Candlestick Charts and Timeframes
  • Learn what a candlestick is -- open, close, high, low on a single period
  • Learn 5 key candlestick patterns: Doji, Hammer, Engulfing (bullish and bearish), Shooting Star
  • Switch between 1-day, 1-week, and 1-hour timeframes on your watched stocks
  • Use TradingView's Bar Replay feature -- travel back 3 months and watch it move forward day by day
  • Journal: write what you notice about patterns before and after price moves
⚡Bar Replay is like a video game. Use it when energy is low -- it keeps you engaged without needing to read.
Week 4
Support, Resistance, and Trend Lines
  • Learn what support is -- a price floor where buyers tend to step in
  • Learn what resistance is -- a price ceiling where sellers tend to push back
  • Draw support and resistance lines on 3 different charts using TradingView's drawing tools
  • Learn what a trend line is and draw one on an uptrend and one on a downtrend
  • Make 5 paper trades this week based only on what you see on the chart. Journal every one with a reason.
Checkpoint 02  ·  End of Week 4
Before moving to Phase 3, you must be able to:
  • Read a candlestick chart and identify at least 3 of the 5 patterns by sight
  • Draw a support line, resistance line, and trend line on any chart
  • Have completed at least 10 total paper trades with journal entries explaining why
  • Used Bar Replay on at least 2 different stocks
3
Tools and Indicators
Weeks 5 & 6  ·  Going Deeper
Practice 🔒 ▼
This phase is locked. Complete Phase 2 and have a parent enter the password to continue.

Indicators help you confirm what you think you see on a chart. The trap most new traders fall into is using too many. Pick two or three and learn them well.

Week 5
Moving Averages and Volume
  • Learn what a moving average is -- the average price over a set period (e.g. 50 days)
  • Add the 20-day and 50-day moving averages to a chart on TradingView
  • Learn what a "golden cross" and "death cross" are
  • Learn what volume tells you -- high volume on a move means more conviction
  • This week: only trade stocks where volume confirms the move you think you see
⚡Set a rule: no more than 2 indicators on a chart at once. More clutter = more confusion = worse decisions.
Week 6
RSI and MACD -- Your Two Best Friends
  • Learn RSI -- above 70 means possibly overbought, below 30 means possibly oversold
  • Learn MACD -- a momentum indicator showing when trends may be gaining or losing strength
  • Add both to a chart and practice spotting buy and sell signals
  • Do a "look-back" exercise: hide the right half of a chart, predict what happened next, then reveal the answer
  • Make 10 paper trades this week combining everything learned so far
Checkpoint 03  ·  End of Week 6  ·  Midpoint Review
This one matters a lot -- midpoint gut-check:
  • Explain RSI and MACD in plain English, no jargon
  • Show the journal: at least 25 paper trades with documented reasons
  • Together review the trades -- what % were winners? What patterns are in the losers?
  • Pass the look-back test: correctly identify trend direction on 3 out of 5 blind charts
4
Risk Management and Strategy
Weeks 7 & 8  ·  The Real Lesson
Analysis 🔒 ▼
This phase is locked. Complete Phase 3 and have a parent enter the password to continue.

This is the phase most people skip -- and the reason most people lose money. Good traders are not the ones who pick the most winners. They control how much they lose when they are wrong.

Week 7
Stop Losses, Position Sizing, and Risk/Reward
  • Learn what a stop loss is -- an automatic exit if the trade goes against you
  • Learn the 1% rule: never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade
  • Learn risk/reward ratio -- if you risk $1, aim to make at least $2
  • Go back and review your paper trade journal -- how many trades had no defined exit plan?
  • From this week forward: every paper trade must have a stop loss and a target price set before entering
⚡The stop loss is not admitting defeat. It is a superpower. It means one bad trade can never ruin your whole account.
Week 8
Building YOUR Strategy
  • Review your best and worst paper trades from the whole summer
  • Write a simple trading plan: what will you look for before entering? What makes you exit?
  • Decide: stocks or crypto? Pick one. Crypto moves faster but is far more volatile.
  • Trade for a full week following only your written plan. No improvising.
  • At week end: did following the plan feel different from winging it?
Checkpoint 04  ·  End of Week 8
The hardest checkpoint -- risk management:
  • Every paper trade from Week 7 onward has a documented stop loss and price target
  • Can explain risk/reward ratio without looking it up
  • Has a written, one-page trading plan with entry rules, exit rules, and risk per trade
  • Has chosen stocks or crypto and can explain why
5
Prove It: The Final Test
Weeks 9 & 10  ·  Earning the Key
Real Money Gate 🔒 ▼
This phase is locked. Complete Phase 4 and have a parent enter the password to continue.

The last two weeks are a live simulation. Full rules, full discipline, full journal. This is not about making money -- it is about proving the habits are there before real money is on the line.

Weeks 9 & 10
Two-Week Live Simulation
  • Trade paper money every market day using only your written trading plan
  • Log every single trade: entry, exit, stop loss, target, and outcome
  • Do not skip days. Consistency is what you are proving.
  • End of each week: review trades together as a parent-child debrief
  • Calculate your win rate and average risk/reward for the two weeks
  • Write a short summary: what did you learn? What would you do differently?
⚡This is the hardest part because it feels the most like a job. That is the point. Trading is a skill, not a shortcut.
Final Checkpoint  ·  End of Week 10  ·  The Real Money Gate
ALL of these must be met before any real money account is opened:
  • 10+ days of consistent paper trading with a full journal
  • Win rate of at least 40% (4 wins out of 10 with good risk management is profitable)
  • Average risk/reward of at least 1:1.5 or better across all trades
  • Written trading plan is complete and has been followed for 2 full weeks
  • Can explain each trade and why to a parent -- no vague answers
  • Agrees in writing to start with a maximum of $200 and strict stop-loss discipline
